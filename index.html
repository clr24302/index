An Introduction to Caroline Rigdon

New Beginnings

I spent the first eight years of my life in Lawrenceville, Georgia, in a neighborhood home that my father built for my mother. My brother, my sister, and I only had a short glimpse into suburban life before our parents uprooted our lives and moved us out to the countryside of Jackson Country, where I spent my elementary school days feeding chickens and playing by the creek. My parents envisioned spending the rest of their lives in this beautiful, enchanting farmhouse, but everything changed when the housing market crashed.

After sustaining substantial financial hardships, my father, a general contractor, had no choice but to declare bankruptcy and move our family back into the confines of a neighborhood. During the nine years I lived in this home, I started middle school, joined marching band, became a disciple, and graduated high school. It was in the living room of this house that I found out I was accepted into the University of Georgia. In my bathroom I primped for proms, concerts, and parties. The kitchen is where my sister introduced her boyfriend who is now her fiance to my parents. Though our living circumstances were born of pain, they still brought immeasurable moments of light into our lives.

In December of 2018, my parents were finally in a place of financial stability to move back onto a plot of land and design their dream house. They now reside there with my 15 year old brother and one year old golden retriever, Gus. Throughout my childhood, I was never once under the impression that everything was not going to be okay. My parents have unfailingly demonstrated faith in the Lord and faith in each other through my entire life, and it has made all the difference in the person I am today.

A Woman On A Mission

In December of 2016, I went on my first mission trip to Nicaragua. There, a team of 30 other Americans and I planted a summer camp in which children from ages 9-18 came from across northern Latin America to learn about the love of God and the spirit of Camp Swamp. We trained a team of Nicaraguans on how to successfully run a camp that has a lasting, positive impact on campers and counselors alike. One year later in 2017 I returned as a camp counselor for the third and final year of training for the Nicaraguans. In May of 2018, I departed on a flight to Cambodia, where we planted a camp using the same training implimented in Nicaragua. Below is a video I shot and edited to document my time there.
